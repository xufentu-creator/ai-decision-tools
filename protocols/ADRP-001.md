## Abstract

The AI Decision Responsibility Protocol (ADRP) defines a structural governance framework for responsibility allocation within AI-mediated decision systems.

As artificial intelligence becomes embedded in enterprise and organizational environments, decision processes increasingly involve automated systems. Without explicit structural governance, responsibility for outcomes may become unclear, leading to accountability gaps and governance risks.

ADRP establishes a minimal protocol for identifying decision points, defining human judgment nodes, and maintaining responsibility traceability in complex socio-technical systems.

This protocol focuses on decision structure and governance mechanisms rather than algorithm design or model performance.

---

## Summary

The AI Decision Responsibility Protocol provides a governance framework addressing responsibility allocation in AI-assisted environments.

Core elements include:

• identification of decision points
• presence of human judgment nodes
• explicit responsibility assignment
• traceable decision pathways

The protocol supports stable governance structures in complex systems where automated processes interact with human decision makers.

## 1. Scope

This protocol applies to AI-mediated decision systems, including:

• enterprise AI decision environments
• automated operational systems
• algorithmic recommendation systems
• AI-supported governance processes
• decision-support platforms

The protocol addresses:

• decision structure
• human judgment participation
• responsibility allocation
• traceable governance pathways

The protocol does not address:

• AI model training methods
• algorithm performance metrics
• software engineering implementation details

## 2. Normative Principles

### 2.1 Responsibility Non-Delegation

Responsibility for consequential decisions SHALL NOT be assigned to automated systems.

AI systems may assist, recommend, or execute actions but cannot serve as responsible entities.

---

### 2.2 Human Judgment as Structural Constraint

Human judgment SHALL exist as a structural constraint in AI-mediated decision processes where contextual evaluation is required.

---

### 2.3 Traceability

All consequential decisions SHALL maintain traceable records linking outcomes to accountable entities.

---

### 2.4 Governance Transparency

Decision structures SHALL maintain transparency sufficient for governance review and audit.

---

### 2.5 Risk Sensitivity

The presence and authority of human judgment nodes SHALL increase with decision risk and impact.

---

## 3. Definitions

### 3.1 AI Decision System

A system in which algorithmic processes generate outputs that influence or determine operational decisions.

---

### 3.2 Decision Point

A stage within a workflow where system outputs affect consequential actions or outcomes.

---

### 3.3 Human Judgment Node

A defined point in a decision structure where a human evaluates, confirms, modifies, or overrides algorithmic outputs.

---

### 3.4 Responsible Entity

The individual or organizational actor accountable for the outcome of a decision.

---

### 3.5 Responsibility Transfer Point

The stage at which operational responsibility shifts between automated systems and human actors.

---

### 3.6 Responsibility Drift

A condition in which responsibility becomes ambiguous due to unclear structural allocation within automated systems.

---

## 4. Structural Requirements

Systems implementing ADRP MUST define the following structural elements.

### 4.1 Decision Point Identification

All decision points SHALL be documented within the system workflow.

---

### 4.2 Human Judgment Nodes

Human judgment nodes SHALL be defined where:

• decisions carry high impact
• outcomes may be irreversible
• contextual evaluation is required
• legal or ethical implications exist

---

### 4.3 Responsibility Assignment

Each decision point SHALL have an explicitly defined responsible entity.

AI systems SHALL NOT be designated as responsible entities.

---

### 4.4 Decision Traceability

Systems SHALL enable reconstruction of:

• input information
• AI-generated outputs
• human judgment interactions
• final decision responsibility

---

## 5. Human Judgment Conditions

Human judgment MUST be present under the following conditions:

• high financial or operational impact
• safety-critical decisions
• strategic organizational decisions
• regulatory or legal implications
• significant uncertainty in AI prediction

---

## 6. Decision Responsibility Mapping

Systems implementing ADRP SHOULD provide a structured mapping of decision roles.

Example:

| Stage           | AI Role      | Human Judgment  | Risk Level | Responsible Entity |
| --------------- | ------------ | --------------- | ---------- | ------------------ |
| Data processing | Automated    | None            | Low        | System operator    |
| Recommendation  | AI generated | Review required | Medium     | Manager            |
| Final decision  | AI assisted  | Mandatory       | High       | Executive          |

---

## 7. Compliance

A system may be considered compliant with ADRP if it satisfies the following conditions:

• decision points are defined
• human judgment nodes are present where required
• responsibility assignments are explicit
• decision pathways are traceable

Failure to satisfy these conditions indicates non-compliance.

It provides constraints that support stable decision governance within complex organizational systems.
