## Abstract

This document defines structural requirements for decision traceability in AI-mediated systems.

As automated processes increasingly influence operational and organizational decisions, the ability to reconstruct how a decision was formed becomes essential for governance, accountability, and system stability.

This standard specifies how decision pathways may be structured so that system inputs, automated outputs, human involvement, and final decisions can be traced and understood.

---

## Summary

The Decision Traceability Standard defines structural principles for recording and reconstructing decision pathways within AI-mediated systems.

It establishes requirements for documenting:

• system inputs
• automated outputs
• human judgment interactions
• final decision responsibility

The purpose is to maintain transparency and accountability in complex decision environments.

---

## 1. Scope

This standard applies to systems in which automated processes contribute to decision formation.

Applicable environments include:

• enterprise decision systems
• AI-assisted operational platforms
• algorithmic recommendation systems
• automated workflow environments
• governance and management systems

The standard focuses on structural traceability rather than technical implementation.

---

## 2. Definition

Decision Traceability refers to the structural ability to reconstruct how a decision outcome was produced.

Traceability includes the identification of:

• information inputs
• system processing outputs
• human judgment interactions
• final decision responsibility

---

## 3. Traceability Structure

A traceable decision pathway typically includes the following elements:

Input Information
→ System Processing
→ AI Output or Recommendation
→ Human Judgment Interaction (if present)
→ Final Decision Outcome

This structure enables reconstruction of the decision process.

---

## 4. Traceable Components

Systems implementing decision traceability SHOULD record:

• relevant input data
• system-generated outputs or recommendations
• human evaluation or modification
• the responsible decision entity

These components enable transparent reconstruction of decision pathways.

---

## 5. Conditions Requiring Traceability

Decision traceability is particularly important when:

• decisions involve high operational impact
• outcomes affect organizational or societal systems
• accountability must be demonstrated
• automated recommendations influence final outcomes
• disputes or reviews may arise

---

## 6. Relationship with ADRP-001

ADRP-001 defines responsibility allocation structures in AI-mediated decision environments.

Decision traceability supports ADRP-001 by enabling verification of responsibility assignments through reconstructable decision pathways.

---

## 7. Relationship with ADRP-002

ADRP-002 defines the structural role of Human Judgment Nodes.

When human judgment nodes are present, traceability should include records of human evaluation, validation, or override actions within the decision pathway.

---

## 8. Structural Flexibility

This standard defines conceptual requirements for decision traceability.

It does not prescribe specific technologies, logging systems, or data storage mechanisms.

Organizations may implement traceability structures using different technical approaches depending on system design and operational context.

---

## 9. Applicability and Non-Binding Nature

This document represents a conceptual governance framework.

It is not legally binding and does not mandate specific system implementations.

Organizations may adapt the principles described here according to operational, technical, and regulatory contexts.

---

## 10. Scope Limitation

• auditing procedures
• legal compliance requirements

These aspects may vary depending on system context.
