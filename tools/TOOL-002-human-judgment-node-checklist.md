# Human Judgment Node Checklist

Tool ID: TOOL-002  
Version: 1.0  
Status: Draft  
Category: Governance Tool  
Publication Date: 2026  

Related Protocol: ADRP-002 — Human Judgment Node Standard  

---

## Overview

The Human Judgment Node Checklist is a governance tool designed to assess whether human judgment is properly integrated within AI-mediated decision systems.

As AI systems increasingly automate decision processes, organizations must ensure that human oversight is not removed or reduced to a passive role. This tool provides a structured method to evaluate the presence, position, and effectiveness of human judgment within decision workflows.

---

## Purpose

This tool enables organizations to:

• identify where human judgment exists in decision processes  
• evaluate whether human involvement is meaningful or superficial  
• ensure critical decisions include human evaluation  
• prevent full automation of high-risk decision points  
• support governance, compliance, and audit requirements  

---

## Scope

This tool applies to:

• AI-assisted decision systems  
• automated workflows with decision impact  
• recommendation systems influencing human action  
• operational, financial, or strategic decision processes  
• environments requiring human oversight or accountability  

---

## Core Concept

A Human Judgment Node is a point in a decision process where a human is required to:

• interpret system outputs  
• apply contextual reasoning  
• evaluate uncertainty or risk  
• approve, modify, or reject outcomes  

The goal is not to include humans everywhere, but to ensure they are present where judgment is necessary.

---

## Checklist

Evaluate each decision workflow using the following criteria:

### 1. Presence

• Does a human judgment node exist in the workflow?  
• At which stage does it occur?  

---

### 2. Position

• Is the human involved before or after AI output?  
• Is the human able to influence the final outcome?  

---

### 3. Authority

• Does the human have decision authority or only review visibility?  
• Can the human override system outputs?  

---

### 4. Information Access

• Does the human have access to relevant data and context?  
• Are system outputs explainable or interpretable?  

---

### 5. Responsibility

• Is the human clearly accountable for the final decision?  
• Is responsibility documented?  

---

### 6. Risk Alignment

• Are human judgment nodes placed at high-risk decision points?  
• Are low-risk decisions appropriately automated?  

---

### 7. Independence

• Is the human decision independent from system bias?  
• Are there safeguards against blind acceptance of AI outputs?  

---

## Assessment Output

Organizations can classify decision workflows as:

• Fully Automated — no human judgment required  
• Human-in-the-Loop — human reviews or approves decisions  
• Human-in-Control — human makes final decisions  
• Undefined — unclear or missing human role  

---

## Expected Outcomes

Applying this tool helps organizations:

• ensure human oversight in critical decisions  
• reduce automation-related risks  
• improve accountability structures  
• align decision processes with governance principles  
• increase trust in AI-assisted systems  

---

## Limitations

This tool does not:

• define how human judgment should be implemented technically  
• replace regulatory or compliance frameworks  
• guarantee correctness of decisions  

It provides a structural evaluation method only.
