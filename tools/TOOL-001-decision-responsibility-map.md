## Overview

The Decision Responsibility Map Builder is a governance tool designed to support the identification, structuring, and documentation of responsibility allocation within AI-mediated decision systems.

As AI systems increasingly participate in operational and strategic decisions, responsibility boundaries often become unclear. This tool provides a structured method to explicitly define how decisions are formed, who is accountable, and where human judgment is required.

---

## Purpose

This tool enables organizations to:

• clarify responsibility allocation in decision processes  
• identify where AI participates in decision-making  
• ensure human accountability is explicitly defined  
• reduce ambiguity in automated or semi-automated workflows  
• support governance, audit, and compliance requirements  

---

## Scope

The tool applies to any system where:

• AI generates recommendations or outputs  
• automated systems influence decisions  
• human operators review, approve, or override outcomes  
• decisions carry operational, financial, or ethical consequences  

---

## Core Concept

A decision responsibility map represents the structure of a decision process, including:

• input sources  
• system processing (AI or automated logic)  
• decision points  
• human judgment nodes  
• final decision authority  
• responsible entities  

The goal is to make responsibility explicit at every stage of the decision flow.

---

## Responsibility Mapping Framework

A typical decision responsibility map includes the following elements:

| Stage | System Role | Human Judgment | Risk Level | Responsible Entity |
|------|------------|----------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Data Input | Data collection | None | Low | System owner |
| Processing | AI analysis | None | Low | Technical team |
| Recommendation | AI-generated output | Review required | Medium | Department manager |
| Decision | AI-assisted | Mandatory | High | Decision authority |
| Execution | Automated or manual | Optional | Medium | Operations team |

---

## Implementation Steps

1. Identify key decision workflows within the organization.

2. Break each workflow into discrete decision stages.

3. Define the role of AI or automated systems at each stage.

4. Determine where human judgment is required.

5. Assign a responsible entity for each decision point.

6. Document the structure using a consistent mapping format.

---

## Output

The output of this tool is a structured representation of decision responsibility, which can be used for:

• governance documentation  
• internal policy alignment  
• audit preparation  
• risk assessment  
• system design review  

---

## Expected Outcomes

Applying this tool helps organizations:

• improve clarity of responsibility  
• strengthen decision accountability  
• reduce governance risks  
• align human oversight with system behavior  
• increase transparency in AI-assisted processes  

---

## Limitations

This tool defines structural responsibility but does not:

• prescribe specific technical implementations  
• replace legal or regulatory requirements  
• provide operational or compliance certification  

Organizations should adapt the framework to their specific context.
