## Abstract

This document defines the structural role of human judgment within AI-mediated decision systems.

As automated processes increasingly contribute to decision-making, human judgment remains necessary for contextual evaluation, responsibility allocation, and governance stability.

This standard specifies how human judgment is represented as a structural component within decision architectures.

---

## Summary

This standard introduces the concept of the Human Judgment Node as a structural element in decision systems.

It defines:

• the role of human judgment within system workflows
• where such nodes appear
• under what conditions they are required
• how they relate to system responsibility structures

---

## 1. Scope

This standard applies to decision systems in which:

• automated processes influence outcomes
• decisions affect real-world operations
• uncertainty or contextual interpretation is present

Applicable environments include:

• enterprise systems
• automated workflows
• decision-support platforms
• governance processes

This standard focuses on structural design and does not specify implementation details.

---

## 2. Definition

A Human Judgment Node is a structural point within a decision system where a human actor evaluates system outputs before final action.

It represents the transition from automated processing to accountable human involvement.

---

## 3. Placement

Human Judgment Nodes may be introduced at:

• high-impact decision points
• irreversible decision stages
• points involving uncertainty
• decisions requiring contextual interpretation
• situations involving ethical or regulatory considerations

The placement of such nodes depends on system design and risk context.

---

## 4. Functional Role

A Human Judgment Node may perform the following functions:

• evaluation of system-generated outputs
• validation of recommendations
• contextual interpretation
• modification or override of system suggestions

These functions enable alignment between system outputs and real-world conditions.

---

## 5. Conditions for Inclusion

Human Judgment Nodes SHOULD be included when:

• decision impact is significant
• uncertainty is present
• system confidence is limited
• accountability must be clearly assigned

---

## 6. Integration in Decision Architecture

Human Judgment Nodes may be integrated into systems as:

• validation checkpoints
• decision review stages
• override mechanisms

They serve to maintain a connection between system outputs and human accountability.

---

## 7. Relationship with ADRP-001

This standard extends ADRP-001.

ADRP-001 defines responsibility structures.
ADRP-002 defines how human judgment is positioned within those structures.
